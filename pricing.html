Common questions

Why are these rates positioned at this level?

Because the results justify them. Pastor Anderson has documented $120M+ in financing facilitated, multiple organizations rebuilt from complete insolvency, and a 20× subscriber growth achievement. Comparable specialists — TMA turnaround managers, fractional C-suite executives, and faith-based strategists of this caliber — command these rates and higher. The question is never the fee. The question is what the engagement produces.

Are there options for organizations that cannot afford these rates?

Yes. The Nonprofit Rescue & Rebuild engagement has a sliding scale for organizations with budgets under $500K. The Mastermind Group provides access at $1,500/month. The Free Executive Planner provides the core frameworks at no cost. And the discovery call is always free — sometimes the conversation alone provides significant clarity.

How quickly can results be expected?

The 90-Day Turnaround Sprint is structured to produce measurable results within the engagement period. Washington Park Association secured $5.5M within months. New England Housing Ministries reached $7.5M cash within 24 months. Individual clients typically identify 3–5 revenue streams they had not previously considered within the first session. Results depend on client execution — the frameworks and strategy are proven.

Do you work with clients outside the United States?

Yes. Pastor Anderson has engaged with leaders across West Africa and internationally. Most engagements are conducted virtually or through a combination of virtual and in-person sessions. International clients are welcome across all service tiers.

Is faith a requirement to engage FaithVision Leadership?

No. While faith is the foundation of Pastor Anderson's personal practice and the source of the strategic principles, the frameworks and results are available to any leader willing to engage seriously with the process. The track record speaks for itself regardless of faith background. Many of the most significant results have been achieved for secular organizations.
